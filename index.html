About

I'm most at home in the gap between low-level hardware constraints and high-level software performance — the place where a timing diagram and a profiler readout end up being the same conversation.

I'm finishing my Master of Engineering in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Oregon State University (Jun 2026), after completing my B.S. there with a minor in Computer Science. My graduate coursework leans toward systems: HPC architecture, modern memory systems, VLSI design, and parallel programming.

I founded and lead the Embedded Systems Design Club at OSU, interned as a Digital Design Engineer at onsemi on automotive image-sensor security, and am spending summer 2026 as a parallel-computing research fellow at Los Alamos National Laboratory.
